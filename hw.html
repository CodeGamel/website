Some fun history about the PB&J

The peanut butter jelly sandwich is a classic childhood dish, and may adults still enjoy it as a throwback meal. The average school child eats about 1500 PB&Js before the end of high school. In 1901 Julia Davis Chandler published the first peanut butter and jelly sandwich recipe in 1901 in the Boston Cooking School Magazine of Culinary Science and Domestic Economics. So today, you will be learning how to make this classic household dish!


COOKING INSTRUCTIONS

Step 1: Collecting your ingredients!

You will need the following:

  • Bread, any type! As long as its bread you and make this!

  • Peanut Butter, we highly recommend Jif Creamy Peanut Butter

  • Jelly, we also highly recommend using a Jam for more experienced PB&J connoisseurs

Step 2: Placing the Peanut Butter!

There is a special technique to this:

  • Ensure you have a secure hand on the butter knife, make sure you stand shoulder-width to enure more stability.

  • With your main index finger on top of the knife, make sure you scoop some peanut butter on that bad boy.

  • Smear the peanut butter delicately on one side of your bread, leave the other side for step 3!

Step 3: Placing the Jelly!

Keep the technique!

  • Just as the step before, assuming you have the proper standing position. Scoop the jelly onto the knife!

  • Smear the jelly delicately on the other side of your bread.

Step 4: Put it all together!

  • Now that you got both pieces of your bread perfectly crafted, just put one on top of the other


  • Be careful not to drop the bread as you may have to go back to Step 1

Step 5: ENJOY!

Don't forget to enjoy your Peanut Butter Jelly Sandwich with a tall glass of milk – the perfect combination!
